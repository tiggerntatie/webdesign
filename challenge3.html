Challenge 3: Create a Movie Review Page

Summary

Use HTML to write a web page review of your favorite movie.

Purpose

This assignment gives you more practice writing web pages “by hand”. You will practice adding anchors to your page (also known as links) and you will write HTML to insert a table. Lastly, you will begin to learn how to add style to your HTML by adding a cascading style sheet (CSS).

Assignment

What is your favorite movie? Using HTML and CSS, write a review of your favorite film. Your review must use some of the tags you learned about last week, plus the new <table> tag for making a table.The table in your review can be used for whatever you like. For example, it could list the major actors in the movie, a short bio of each, and mention a list of movies they have been in. For example:

ActorBioMovies
Lindsay Lohan Lindsay Dee Lohan was born in New York City on 2 July 1986 to Dina Lohan and Michael Lohan. (Credit: IMDB) Freaky Friday, The Parent Trap
Bill Cosby William H. Cosby Jr. was born on July 12th, 1937, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. (Credit: IMDB) Uptown Saturday Night, California Suite

Support your movie review with at least 6 links to other web pages (i.e. supporting information at IMDB, or other movie reviews).

Here are the things you have to do for the assignment:

  1. Set up TextWrangler with a bookmark for netdenizen.org.
  2. In TextWrangler open a new SFTP browser (File/New/SFTP/FTP Browser), pick the netdenizen.org bookmark you created and click New... to create a new file called challenge03.html - this exact name is important!
  3. Your movie review HTML should have examples of the HTML elements we will look at this week (and some from last week):
    • <title> Remember: this goes between the <head> and </head> tags.
    • <h1>, <h2>, etc. (at least one). These are good for titles and sub-titles on the page.
    • <p> put your review text between these.
    • <ul> and <li> (for a list of awards and accolades received by the movie)
    • <a> tag for linking to other pages (at least six)
    • <table> and <tr>, <th> and <td> tags for your table
  4. Add a style sheet (CSS) to customize the font style and background color of the page.
  5. Add a “Valid” icon to the bottom of your page.
  6. Your page should validate with no errors or warnings.
