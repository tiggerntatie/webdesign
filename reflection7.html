Web Design Reflection 7: Typography

Compare this reflection to the last reflection assignment. Do you see any differences in style between this page and the previous one?

The purpose of this reflection is to start you thinking about the choices you make on font size and style. Just as in the last reflection about color theory, there are many people out there who will tell you what to do. The good news is that you get to make up your own mind!

Instructions

  1. Create a web page in your public folder and name it reflection07.html.
  2. Read this discussion of typography on the web. (yes, all of it).
  3. Please answer the following questions in your page, using complete, spell checked sentences:
    • How would you describe the typeface used in the body text of this page, versus the typeface used in the last reflection assignment?
    • How would you describe the typeface used in the title text of this page, versus the typeface used in the title text of the last reflection assignment?
    • If one of the purposes of web design is to communicate ideas and information to your visitors then surely the choice of typeface (font) must be very important. Look again at the last reflection assignment and then back at this one. Which of these two pages is easier to read from the standpoint of text clarity and readability (as opposed to my tortured syntax and writing style)? I am asking here for your opinion but you must back up your opinion with an explanation.
    • In your opinion should web pages strive to resemble print media (magazines and newspapers)? Please back up your opinion with an explanation of why.
    • Based on what you have read, and your own opinion, how should you decide when to use serif or sans-serif fonts on your web page? If you don't know the difference between these then go back and do the reading.
    • Why would you want to specify more than one font in a font-family attribute in your CSS?
  4. Your reflection web page should validate (of course!).
