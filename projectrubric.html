Assessment Standards for HHS 2011 Web Design Final Project
Do Something (up to 50%) Submit a web page of some kind.
More Pages (up to 65%) Multiple pages with content at 3% per page
Essentials (up to 75%)
  • Credit and URL for all images that you did not create. Do not provide a google search URL. Make a reasonable effort to identify the author/artist responsible or indicate that it is no longer covered by copyright (i.e. for very old images). 2%
  • Credit for any 3rd party code (who is the author and where is it posted). 2%
  • If an image is very important to your site (e.g. the site logo, page background, etc.) you must obtain explicit permission from the author. 2%
  • If you use multiple images from one author or source, you must obtain explicit permission from the author to use those images on your site. 2%
  • Your HTML and CSS must validate. I may make an exception for HTML or CSS provided by a 3rd party on a case by case basis. 2%
(In all cases, an e-mail from a webmaster, or individual will be sufficient evidence of permission.)
Excellence (up to 100%)

These will be added to your score if all the essential requirements are met first.

Coding and Adherence to Web Standards

  • All XHTML and CSS validate. 5%
  • CSS and XHTML are used appropriately for style and content, respectively. 1%
  • XHTML elements are used appropriately (i.e. ul or ol for lists and numbered lists of things, table for information in tabular form, etc.). 1%
  • CSS and XHTML are formatted for easy understanding in a text editor. 1%

Functionality

  • All images have sensible alt text. 1%
  • All links work correctly. 1%
  • All images are sized (width by height), compressed appropriately (i.e. choose the lowest acceptable quality for jpg images) and are in an appropriate format (jpg for photos, png for graphics).2%

Design

  • Author has paid attention to typeface size and style, color and background color or image, resulting in clear, readable text. Multiple typefaces are specified to ensure graceful degradation if the desired font is not installed on the visitorâ€™s computer.2%
  • Site home page clearly identifies the name and purpose of site and establishes a theme for the entire site. This can be accomplished with a banner and/or logo. 1%
  • The visitor receives ample support and cues to help with navigating between pages on the site. This can consist of a navigation bar, navigation menu, etc., on every page of the site.1%
  • All pages in the site are stylistically consistent with the home page. In other words, is it obvious to the user that they are on YOUR site, no matter which page they are on? 1%
  • Site and page layouts respond well to different browser sizes (try resizing your browser window - does the design still hold together?) 2%

Content

  • Content is sufficient to meet the purpose of each page. 1%
  • Content is sufficient to meet the purpose of each page. 1%
  • Site identifies its authors and provides contact info. 1%
